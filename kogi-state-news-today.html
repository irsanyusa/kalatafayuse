Kogi state is located in the north central part of Nigeria. It is bordered by the states of Nasarawa to the northeast, Benue to the east, Enugu, Anambra, and Delta to the south, Ondo, Ekiti, and Kwara to the west, and Niger to the north.

Abuja Federal Capital Territory also borders Kogi to the north. The state is nicknamed the “Confluence State'' because the confluence of the River Niger and River Benue occurs next to its capital, Lokoja.

Population

Of the 36 states of Nigeria, Kogi is the 13th largest in area and 20th most populous with an estimated population of about 4.5 million as of 2016.

History

Kogi state was formed from parts of Benue, Niger, and Kwara states on August 27, 1991.

University

Prince Abubakar Audu University (formerly Kogi State University) is located at Anyigba. It was established in 1999 by Prince Abubakar Audu, the former governor of the state.
