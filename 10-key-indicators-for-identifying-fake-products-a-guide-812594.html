10 Key Indicators for Identifying Fake Products: A Guide

Publish date: 2024-07-05

In today’s world, counterfeit products are becoming increasingly prevalent, and it’s not always easy to spot them. However, there are several ways to ensure that you’re purchasing authentic products. Here are 10 key indicators to help you identify fake products.

1. The Chanel Classic flap bag

Chanel’s Classic flap bag is a sought-after fashion accessory worldwide. Unfortunately, counterfeit versions are flooding the market.

To identify an authentic bag, check the quilted leather pattern, which should be subtle and not overly puffy. A fake bag may have an exaggerated quilted pattern. Additionally, ensure that the flap is perfectly aligned with the bottom layer.

2. The Ordinary AHA 30% + BHA 2% peeling solution

The Ordinary’s AHA 30% + BHA 2% peeling solution is a popular product that is often counterfeited. To identify a fake product, pay attention to the scent. An authentic product has a tart scent, while a fake one may smell sweet and soapy.

3. The Air Jordan 11 Retro Cherry

To ensure that you’re buying genuine shoes, examine the stitching and cut of the shoe. The left shoe of a fake pair will have less precise stitching and fewer clean cuts than the right shoe of an authentic pair.

4. Virgin Marula Luxury Facial Oil

When purchasing Virgin Marula Oil, check the color. An authentic bottle should have a specific shade of yellow that matches the brand’s standards. If the oil appears to be a different shade of yellow or is too light or too dark, it may be a counterfeit.

5. Cosrx Advanced Snail 96 Mucin Power Essence

To identify a genuine product, pay attention to the smell and texture. An authentic product should have no distinct scent and a slimy, viscous texture. If the product has a noticeable scent, it may be a fake.

6. Granactive Retinoid 2% Emulsion

When inspecting the label of a product like Granactive Retinoid 2% Emulsion, look for any spelling errors, misalignments, or inconsistencies — genuine products have precisely aligned labels without any of these things. Check to see if the graphics and text are centered and that there is consistency in its design elements.

7. Dior x Air Jordan 1

When purchasing branded shoes, like Nike, look for inconsistencies in the thickness of the text on the logo. The letters on fake shoes tend to be thicker than on genuine models. Also, examine the stitching on the midsole, which will be thinner on the fake shoes and thicker on the genuine ones.

8. AirPods

Examine the packaging and labeling of a product carefully for spelling or capitalization errors. In counterfeit products, the word “AirPods” may be miscapitalized as “Airpods.”

9. Pokémon Pearl Version

To identify counterfeit copies of products, like Pokémon Pearl Version, carefully examine the packaging and labeling. The fake copy may appear darker than the genuine one, and the Pokémon company logo on the fake copy may be super thin.

10. iPhone

When purchasing an iPhone, check the screen quality. An authentic iPhone will have a vibrant and high-quality display, whereas a fake one may have a washed-out appearance with dull colors.
