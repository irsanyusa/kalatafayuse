Jim Carrey Gives the Weirdest Red Carpet Interview and Fans Are Seriously Worried About His Me

Publish date: 2024-07-18

This interview produced more questions than it was supposed to answer. After a long break from the limelight, Jim Carrey finally appeared in public and left everyone speechless. The interviewer was left completely gobsmacked as she couldn’t understand what was going on with Jim. Everyone thought that the actor had some serious mental health issues.

He attended Harper’s Bazaar’s Icons Party.

His behavior was unusual.

The actor started the interview by circling the interviewer. Then, when he was asked if he had a date, the actor replied, “I wanted to find the most meaningless thing that I could come to and join, and here I am.” The interviewer defended the party by saying it celebrated icons. Carrey replied, “That’s the absolute lowest aiming possibility that they could come up with.”

He said he didn’t believe in icons.

He told the interviewer he didn’t believe in icons and pointed at the interviewer stating, “I don’t believe you exist, but there’s a wonderful fragrance in the air.” The woman reminded him that he was an icon too, and Jim started singing a song in response.

He started telling the interviewer about his life philosophy.

Once he finished the song, the actor continued, “I believe that peace lies beyond personality, beyond invention of the skies. I believe we’re a field of energy, dancing for itself.” The interviewer’s jaw dropped. Later Carrey said, “This is not our world. We don’t matter, that’s the good news.”

People started to worry.

Some expressed their concerns on Twitter, “I really hope Jim Carrey isn’t depressed.” Another user, however, supported the actor, “She’s taking herself much too seriously. C’mon! It’s the funny, artistic Jim Carrey, just going with the flow would have made the interview.”

Jim explained his behavior.

Invision/Invision/East News

Later in another interview, Jim explained, “If you go deep into characters, you suddenly have this separation and go, ’Who’s Jim Carrey? He doesn’t exist, actually.’ There’s just a relative manifestation of consciousness appearing, and someone gave him a name, a religion, a nationality. He clustered those together into something that’s supposed to be a personality, and it doesn’t actually exist.”
